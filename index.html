Is my child proficient?

By: Center for Assessment

Each year you receive a report describing your child’s performance on the state assessment in English language arts, mathematics, and perhaps science.

A scale score and achievement level classification describing your child’s performance are prominently displayed at the top of the report. The achievement level names often convey a sense of whether your child is meeting the state’s expectations for students at that grade level. Achievement level descriptions provide some information about what students performing at each level know and are able to do.

The report also contains a great deal of information about the imprecision in test scores and cautions about interpreting a single test score or achievement level classification. After reading the report, you are still left with the question – Is my child Proficient? Or Has my child met the state’s expectations?
